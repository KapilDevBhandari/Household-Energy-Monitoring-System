Sustainable Energy
Sustainable energy refers to energy sources that can meet the needs of the present generation without compromising the ability of future generations to meet their own needs, typically derived from naturally replenishing resources like solar, wind, and hydropower, causing minimal environmental harm.Â 
Why conserve energy?
We conserve energy to protect the environment, reduce costs, and ensure a sustainable future. By using less energy, we lower greenhouse gas emissions, slow down climate change, and preserve natural resources. Conservation also reduces electricity and fuel expenses, making it beneficial for both individuals and businesses.
Role of government in SDG
Governments play a crucial role in promoting sustainable energy by implementing policies, regulations, and incentives that encourage the adoption of renewable energy sources. They provide subsidies and tax incentives to make solar, wind, and other clean energy technologies more affordable for individuals and businesses.

Solar energy
Solar energy works by converting sunlight into electricity using photovoltaic (PV) cells found in solar panels. These cells, typically made of semiconductor materials like silicon, absorb sunlight and trigger the photovoltaic effect, where photons (light particles) excite electrons, creating an electric current. .
